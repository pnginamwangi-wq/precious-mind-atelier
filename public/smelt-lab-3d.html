Why alloy at all?

Pure gold measures 2.5 to 3 on the Mohs hardness scale. A wedding ring in 24K would deform within a year of daily wear. Alloying is not dilution; it is engineering. The copper and silver perform the structural work so the gold can perform the shining.

Colour is a ratio, not a metal

There is no element called rose gold. An 18K rose ring and an 18K yellow ring contain exactly the same 75 percent gold; only the composition of the remaining quarter differs. A professional never prices by colour, only by fineness and weight.

Trust the stamp, not the word

Carat is rounded generously in conversation; the hallmark is not. A stamp of 750 means 750 parts per thousand, tested and struck. If a piece carries no hallmark at all, value it as one standard below whatever is claimed.
