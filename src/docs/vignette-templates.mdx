# Vignette Layouts

Copy-paste MDX templates for composing full editorial vignettes with the
`Vignette`, `Figure`, `Caption`, `PullQuote`, `ChapterMark`, and `Aside`
blocks from `@/components/luxury`.

Every template assumes a page wrapper:

<Source language="tsx" code={`import { Section, Container } from "@/components/luxury";
import {
  Vignette,
  Figure,
  Caption,
  PullQuote,
  ChapterMark,
  Aside,
} from "@/components/luxury";`} />

---

## 01. Single vignette (workhorse)

A 12-col split, image left, caption right. Use for object studies, faculty
profiles, institute highlights.

<Source language="tsx" code={`<Section>
  <Container>
    <Vignette
      index="01"
      image="/images/object-01.jpg"
      alt="Bronze study, Rome, 1971"
      title="A bronze study in restraint"
      origin="Rome, 1971"
      note="One of six castings held privately. The patina is deliberately unlacquered."
      href="/objects/bronze-01"
    />
  </Container>
</Section>`} />

## 02. Alternating spread

Two vignettes with the second reversed. Establishes rhythm without a header
between them.

<Source language="tsx" code={`<Section>
  <Container className="space-y-32 md:space-y-48">
    <Vignette
      index="01"
      image="/images/study-a.jpg"
      alt="Manuscript detail"
      title="A grammar of ornament"
      origin="Florence, 1489"
      note="From the Laurenziana archive, digitised in partnership with the Institute."
      href="/journal/grammar-of-ornament"
    />
    <Vignette
      reverse
      index="02"
      image="/images/study-b.jpg"
      alt="Marble fragment"
      title="Fragment as first draft"
      origin="Attica, 4th c. BCE"
      note="Held at the Institute's Athens residency, viewable by appointment."
      href="/journal/fragment-first-draft"
    />
  </Container>
</Section>`} />

## 03. Chapter opener

Chapter mark, pull quote, then the vignette. Use to open a long-form entry.

<Source language="tsx" code={`<Section>
  <Container narrow className="space-y-20 md:space-y-28">
    <ChapterMark index="III" eyebrow="Chapter" title="On the discipline of looking" />
    <PullQuote
      quote="Attention, sustained past the point of comfort, is the whole of the method."
      attribution="Elena Marchetti"
      role="Director, Institute of Precious Intelligence"
    />
  </Container>
  <Container className="mt-32">
    <Vignette
      index="01"
      image="/images/lecture-hall.jpg"
      alt="Empty lecture hall at dawn"
      title="The first hour"
      origin="Villa Adriana, 06:12"
      note="Before students arrive, the room is set: one object, one lamp, one chair."
    />
  </Container>
</Section>`} />

## 04. Vignette with aside

Pair a vignette with a bordered `Aside` for context, glossary, or footnote.

<Source language="tsx" code={`<Section>
  <Container>
    <div className="grid gap-16 md:grid-cols-12">
      <div className="md:col-span-8">
        <Vignette
          index="04"
          image="/images/atelier.jpg"
          alt="Atelier interior"
          title="The atelier as instrument"
          origin="Milan, 2024"
          note="Six-month residency, three students per cohort, one master."
          href="/programmes/atelier"
        />
      </div>
      <div className="md:col-span-4 md:pt-24">
        <Aside eyebrow="Glossary">
          <strong className="text-ivory">Atelier.</strong> A working studio in
          which mastery is transmitted by proximity rather than instruction.
        </Aside>
      </div>
    </div>
  </Container>
</Section>`} />

## 05. Triptych

Three figures in a row for a gallery moment. Use when the images together
form the argument, and no single caption dominates.

<Source language="tsx" code={`<Section>
  <Container>
    <div className="grid gap-6 md:grid-cols-3 md:gap-8">
      <Figure src="/images/triptych-a.jpg" alt="Study I" aspect="portrait" caption="I. Approach" />
      <Figure src="/images/triptych-b.jpg" alt="Study II" aspect="portrait" caption="II. Encounter" />
      <Figure src="/images/triptych-c.jpg" alt="Study III" aspect="portrait" caption="III. Return" />
    </div>
  </Container>
</Section>`} />

---

## Do

- Keep one vignette per screenful. Rhythm comes from air, not density.
- Alternate `reverse` in long lists so the eye traces a Z, not a column.
- Use `ChapterMark` between vignettes when the subject shifts.

## Don't

- Stack more than three vignettes without a `PullQuote` or `ChapterMark` break.
- Mix `aspect` ratios inside a triptych. Consistency is the composition.
- Add a `href` on every vignette. Reserve the arrow link for the one you want clicked.
