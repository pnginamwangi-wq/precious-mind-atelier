# Caption Layouts

Copy-paste MDX templates for composing standalone `Caption` blocks around
figures, media, and quiet moments. Captions carry the editorial voice when
a full `Vignette` would feel heavy.

Every template assumes:

<Source language="tsx" code={`import { Section, Container } from "@/components/luxury";
import { Caption, Figure, Hairline } from "@/components/luxury";`} />

---

## 01. Caption beside a figure

Manual 12-col split. Use when you need finer control than `Vignette` allows
(different aspect, custom media, video, embed).

<Source language="tsx" code={`<Section>
  <Container>
    <div className="grid items-center gap-10 md:grid-cols-12 md:gap-16">
      <div className="md:col-span-7">
        <Figure
          src="/images/portrait-01.jpg"
          alt="Portrait of the master"
          aspect="portrait"
        />
      </div>
      <div className="md:col-span-5">
        <Caption
          index="01"
          title="A hand that remembers"
          origin="Kyoto, 2023"
          note="Forty years of lacquer, taught in silence, one apprentice at a time."
          href="/faculty/kyoto-lacquer"
          cta="Read the profile"
        />
      </div>
    </div>
  </Container>
</Section>`} />

## 02. Caption over a full-bleed image

Absolutely positioned caption on a hero-scale figure. Use for a chapter cover
or a landing moment.

<Source language="tsx" code={`<Section className="!py-0">
  <div className="relative h-[80vh] w-full overflow-hidden bg-charcoal">
    <img
      src="/images/hero-cover.jpg"
      alt="Interior, dawn"
      className="h-full w-full object-cover"
    />
    <div className="pointer-events-none absolute inset-0 bg-gradient-to-t from-obsidian via-obsidian/40 to-transparent" />
    <Container className="absolute inset-x-0 bottom-0 pb-16 md:pb-24">
      <div className="max-w-xl">
        <Caption
          index="I"
          title="The room before the lesson"
          origin="Villa Adriana"
        />
      </div>
    </Container>
  </div>
</Section>`} />

## 03. Caption pair (dialogue)

Two captions side by side, one left-aligned, one right-aligned. Use to set
two objects, two artists, or two ideas in conversation.

<Source language="tsx" code={`<Section>
  <Container>
    <div className="grid gap-16 md:grid-cols-2 md:gap-24">
      <Caption
        index="A"
        title="The maker"
        origin="Milan, 1962"
        note="Trained in the workshop of his father. Never signed his own name."
        align="left"
      />
      <Caption
        index="B"
        title="The keeper"
        origin="Paris, 2019"
        note="Acquired the archive in 2019. First public showing here."
        align="right"
      />
    </div>
  </Container>
</Section>`} />

## 04. Caption stack (list of works)

A vertical list of captions separated by hairlines. Use for a catalogue,
programme notes, or a reading list.

<Source language="tsx" code={`<Section>
  <Container narrow>
    <ul className="divide-y divide-platinum/10">
      {[
        { index: "01", title: "On restraint", origin: "Essay, 2024", href: "/journal/on-restraint" },
        { index: "02", title: "Fragment as first draft", origin: "Essay, 2024", href: "/journal/fragment" },
        { index: "03", title: "The discipline of looking", origin: "Lecture, 2023", href: "/journal/looking" },
      ].map((item) => (
        <li key={item.index} className="py-10 first:pt-0 last:pb-0">
          <Caption
            index={item.index}
            title={item.title}
            origin={item.origin}
            href={item.href}
            cta="Read"
          />
        </li>
      ))}
    </ul>
  </Container>
</Section>`} />

## 05. Caption without a figure (colophon)

Caption used as a standalone editorial mark to close a chapter, credit a
photographer, or open a footer.

<Source language="tsx" code={`<Section>
  <Container narrow>
    <Caption
      index="Colophon"
      title="On this edition"
      origin="Set in Söhne Buch and Canela Deck"
      note="Photography by Anna Rausch. Printed in Verona on Munken Pure 120gsm."
      align="left"
    />
  </Container>
</Section>`} />

---

## Do

- Keep captions under four lines. If more is needed, promote to a `Vignette` or prose block.
- Match `align="right"` when the caption sits to the right of media, so the hairline anchors the eye.
- Use the `index` slot consistently across a page (all numeric, all roman, or all letters, never mixed).

## Don't

- Use `Caption` for body prose. It has its own rhythm and will fight paragraphs.
- Include more than one `href` per caption. The arrow link is a single, deliberate exit.
- Pair a caption with a decorative image. If the image doesn't earn a title, drop the caption.
